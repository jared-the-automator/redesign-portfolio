Handcrafted Construction

Built the Way They Have Always Been Built

Every canoe starts with carefully harvested birch bark and white cedar, lashed and sealed using traditional methods passed down through generations. The result is a vessel that is light, strong, and alive with the character of natural materials.

01

Hand-selected Materials

Birch bark and white cedar chosen for grain, flexibility, and durability in real water conditions.

02

Traditional Methods

Root lashing, spruce gum sealing, and hand-pegged ribs built exactly as they have been for centuries.

03

Custom Specifications

Each canoe is built to order, sized and styled for the paddler and the waters they will travel.

The Builder

Tom Byers, Author and Canoe Builder

Tom Byers is an author and birchbark canoe builder dedicated to keeping the ancient craft alive. Through commissioned builds, public workshops, and cultural revitalization programs, he works to make sure this knowledge does not disappear.

His canoes have been built for private collectors, museums, and cultural institutions. Each vessel carries the marks of its maker and the materials from which it came.

The Author

Canoe Dreams

Tom Byers is the author of Canoe Dreams, a book that shares the stories, techniques, and cultural significance of birchbark canoe building. Drawing from decades of experience on the workshop floor and on the water, the book captures why this craft still matters.
