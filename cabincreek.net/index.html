The Work

01

Period Technique

Skelp-welded wrought iron barrels. Hand-forged locks. All hardware forged on-site in an 18th century manner, just as it was done 250 years ago.

02

Documented From the Start

Before fabrication begins, each project is researched and documented for historical correctness. Every screw, lock bolt, inlay, and fitting is accounted for.

03

60 Combined Years

Brad and Shane Emig have been building together since the early 1990s. Their Arms Making Workshops have trained students to build period-accurate rifles from raw stock.

Setting the Standard
Since 1985

The Yorktown Rifle has been in production since 1985. It set the standard for quality and authenticity at a price that makes this craft accessible. Since then, the shop has developed the Pennsylvania Mountain Rifle, Colonial Virginia Rifle, Colonial American Fowler, Colonial American Pistol, and the Boys and Ladies Rifle.

Each model is based on a documented surviving example. The Colonial American Fowler derives from a gun believed made by John Newcomer in Hempfield Township, Lancaster County, circa 1760-1780. The documentation matters to them.
