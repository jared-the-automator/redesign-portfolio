Three ways to ride
the High Sierra

01

Daily Trail Rides

Half-day and full-day horseback rides from the McGee Creek pack station, June through September. All experience levels. Reservations by phone, two-person minimum.

02

Backcountry Pack Trips

Multi-day expeditions to high country lakes and granite meadows. Access into the John Muir Wilderness, Kings Canyon and Sequoia National Parks. Mules carry your camp.

03

Drop Camps and Dunnage

Pack mules carry your camping or backpacking gear while you hike in on your own pace. Spot trips to named high country destinations, or custom multi-night base camps.

The Sierra doesn't stop
in November

When McGee Creek closes for the high season, rides move to the Alabama Hills outside Lone Pine and the Tungsten Hills near Bishop. Different terrain, same horses and mules.

Two to three hours, scenic stops for photos, trailers meeting you at the trailhead. Available October through April, starting with the Lone Pine Film Festival. 48-hour advance reservation required. Group discounts available.
