The work

One sculptor, three materials.

Every piece starts from the same anatomy study, then moves into the medium that suits it. Collectors buy across all three.

01

Bronze

Lost-wax bronze editions and one-off portrait castings, finished and patinated in the studio.

02

Bone china

Hand-cast bone china horses, a fragile medium few sculptors work in, each piece finished by hand.

03

Artist resins

Limited cold-cast resin releases for the model horse hobby, including pieces shown at Breyerfest.

From clay to patina

Built on the armature, refined to the muscle.

Each horse is sculpted over a wire and steel armature, the proportions checked against real conformation before a mold is ever pulled.

The unfinished stage is where the anatomy is decided. Only when the pose reads true does the piece move toward bronze, china, or resin.
