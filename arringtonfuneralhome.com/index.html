A Legacy of Firsts in Birmingham

In 1990, Rachel R. Arrington became the first African-American woman to build and operate a funeral home in Birmingham. What began as a long-held dream became a cornerstone of the community.

After earning her degree in Mortuary Science from Jefferson State Junior College, Mrs. Arrington designed and constructed a new facility on Cotton Avenue in southwest Birmingham. Her vision was clear: create a space where families feel supported, respected, and understood.

Today, Arrington Funeral Home continues that mission with a dedicated team of seven full-time and fifteen part-time employees, all committed to providing the highest level of service and care.

"Strive every day to do some good, have a purpose for living and make a difference in everything you do."

- Rachel R. Arrington, Founder
