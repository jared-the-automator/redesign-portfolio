Five Rivers

Each river is a different conversation

01

The Beaverhead

A technical tailwater with deep pools and steady hatches. The browns here are smart and worth every cast.

02

The Big Hole

Legendary dry fly water. Wide meadows, long runs, and a salmonfly hatch that brings the whole river to the surface.

03

The Madison

Classic Montana freestone. Pocket water, riffles, and miles of wadeable access framed by the Madison Range.

Plus the Jefferson and Ruby — ask about both

Seven guides who know every bend and hatch

Brad Heine, David Blanton, Eric Shores, Garey Avis, Hadley Snortland, Holly Phipps, and John Hudgens. Between them, decades on these rivers.

They rig the boats at dawn, tie on what the river is asking for, and know when to move and when to wait. Some days are about the fishing. Some are about the water and the light.

Every guide rows their own drift boat. No trainee trips, no group shuttles. One boat, one guide, one or two anglers.

Riverside cabins at the Stonefly Inn

Stay where you fish. Six cabins on the property, each a short walk from the boat ramp. Coffee on the porch while the fog lifts off the river.
