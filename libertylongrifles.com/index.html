Curly maple, selected for figure

Each blank is chosen for the highest natural curl. Stocks are finished with up to ten coats of oil-varnish, hand-rubbed between each coat and finished with optional aging for warmth.

02

American-made hardware throughout

Rice barrels. Jim Chambers locks. Butt plates and trigger guards sand- or wax-cast in-shop. All engraving done with the traditional chasing hammer and chisel, consistent with arms from the 17th and 18th centuries.

Faithful to the historical school

Whether you want a Fleeger-pattern Allegheny County rifle or a Herman Rupp Lehigh Valley piece, each reproduction follows the documented characteristics of that school without shortcut or compromise.

A second generation
in the tradition.

Marvin Kemper learned the craft from his father, Cornell Kemper, who built Kentucky longrifles in Ferdinand, Indiana for nearly fifty years. When Ferdinand celebrated its 175th anniversary in 2015, the community commissioned Marvin for the commemorative rifle.

In 2016, the Grouseland Foundation authorized Marvin to build the official reproduction of the Indiana State Rifle for the state's bicentennial. That piece is now the recorded standard for the "Grouseland Rifle."

Every piece from Liberty Longrifles is built to the customer's exact specifications. In some cases that means copying an original arm in every documented detail. In others it means custom elements designed with Marvin's input.

Commissioning a piece

A custom rifle from Liberty Longrifles starts at $5,000 and runs to $10,000 depending on the level of embellishment. After a conversation about your preferences, Marvin sends a written proposal covering specifications, price, and an anticipated completion timeline.

Most customers come with a school in mind: Lehigh Valley, Lancaster, Allegheny County, early Virginia. Those who don't are welcome to start in the gallery. Either way, Marvin will ask the right questions and offer suggestions based on what he's built before.

Phone calls and shop visits are welcome. Email works too. The conversation comes first.
