A Living Legacy

From Hunter to Guardian: The Nanan Story

Simon Oudit Nanan lived in Cunupia, close to the Caroni Swamp. He farmed part-time and worked the sugar cane plantation, but he also hunted and fished in the swamp. In the early 1930s, he began taking British aristocrats and French Creole families on tours into the mangroves.

Simon and his son Winston grew concerned about the Scarlet Ibis. In 1948, they collected over 200 signatures and petitioned the Conservator of Forests. The Caroni Bird Sanctuary was created, and the Scarlet Ibis gained protection. When Trinidad and Tobago became independent in 1962, the ibis was named the national bird and given total protection.

Under Winston Nanan's stewardship, the Caroni Swamp became a premier tourist attraction, known throughout the world. Today, the Nanan family continues to share this sanctuary with visitors from every continent.
