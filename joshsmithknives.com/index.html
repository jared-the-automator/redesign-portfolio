The Story

Fifth-Generation
Montanan

Raised in Lincoln, Montana just outside the Bob Marshall Wilderness. Forged a knife from chainsaw chain at age 11. Became the youngest Journeyman Smith in American Bladesmith Society history at age 15, and the youngest Master Smith at 19.

Thirty years later, every blade still comes out of the same_pf_quep shop on 21 acres in Frenchtown, tested against the grain of a Montana pine log.

The Shop

Frenchtown,
Montana

A 21-acre shop on the outskirts of Missoula County. The forge runs year-round, burning natural gas and coal to reach the temperatures needed for forge-welding Damascus and heat-treating high-alloy steels. Every blade that leaves the shop has been in my hands from raw stock to finished edge.

The work is backed by a lifetime of training, starting with Rick Dunkerley in Lincoln and extending through the ABS journeyman and master smith programs.
