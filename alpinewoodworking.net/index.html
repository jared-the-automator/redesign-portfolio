The Process

From Blank to Bank

The wood starts as a rough sawn blank. The handle is shaped on a lathe, then the hoop is steam-bent into an oval and fixed with pegs, not glue. The mesh is knotted by hand around the rim.

What matters is the balance. A net that feels right in your hand does not need hype. It needs weight in the right places and a rim thin enough to slide under the fish without spooking it.
