What we fish

Every kind of water
within reach.

01

Inshore and Flats

Red drum on topwater in the marsh creeks, speckled trout in the sounds, tailing fish on the flats for fly anglers. The 19' Pathfinder keeps it shallow and quiet.

02

Ocean and Offshore

Cobia, grouper, wahoo, sailfish, and blackfin tuna on the 2023 Contender 28T. Departs Town Creek Marina, Slip 65, Beaufort. Nearshore and bluewater.

03

False Albacore and Fly

Cape Lookout false albacore on fly or light tackle, September through November. When the albies show up on bait balls off the inlet, there's nothing like it on the East Coast.

Meet the captain

Capt. Tom Roller.

Tom has been fishing the Crystal Coast since before most guides were born. More than 30 years on these specific inlets, creeks, and offshore grounds. He has been guiding full time for 16 of those years.

He runs a two-boat operation: a 19' Pathfinder Fusion for flats and inshore work, and a 2023 Contender 28T for nearshore and offshore. Whatever is biting, he has the right platform for it.

He serves on the Coastal Conservation Association, pushing back against overharvest on the NC coast. When you book with him, you're fishing with someone who is fighting to keep this fishery alive.

What to know before you book.

Inshore trips depart various Beaufort boat ramps. Offshore leaves from Town Creek Marina, Slip 65, 114 Town Creek Drive.

Peak inshore months: August, September, and October. False albacore runs September through November.

Groups up to four adults. Larger groups with kids can often be accommodated. Half-day and full-day options.

Spin and fly anglers both welcome. All tackle provided, or bring your own rods if you prefer.
