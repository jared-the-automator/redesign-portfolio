What goes into every blade

alike. Ever.

01

Damascus Pattern Steel

Each blade is pattern-welded from layered steel. The wavy grain running through the metal is structural, not decorative. It's the same technique bladesmiths have used for centuries, done by hand at the forge.

02

Handle Fitted to the Work

Stag, ironwood, Kirinite, bone. Cowboy matches the handle to how you'll use the knife. A hunting blade that rides your belt gets different scales than a presentation piece headed for a display case.

03

Sized for Your Hand

Before steel gets cut, Cowboy learns what you need from the knife: field dressing game, daily carry, or something to pass to your son in twenty years. Custom means measured, not just marked up.

The man behind the anvil

Cowboy
Szymanski

Cowboy moved to Bellville in 2003 to restore and operate the town's 1891 blacksmith shop. He has been at the forge ever since, through five seasons, a relocated shop, and a slot on History Channel's Forged in Fire.

In 2018 he and his wife started the Texas Select Custom Cutlery Event. It now draws more than 200 vendors and buyers from across the country and beyond.

Every knife that leaves his shop is one of a kind. The design, the steel, the handle. Nothing is repeated.
