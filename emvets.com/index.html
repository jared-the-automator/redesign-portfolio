A Legacy in Northeastern Pennsylvania

Started with a horse.
Built over a century.

In 1916, Dr. Isaac V. Stoll graduated from the University of Pennsylvania School of Veterinary Medicine and returned to Rome. He hitched a road cart to his father's colt and drove out to treat a horse in the Orwell community. That was the first call. First-year fees: $1,500.

What started as horses became dairy herds as farms in Bradford County shifted. The practice expanded to a 50-mile radius. Partners joined, sons graduated from veterinary school, and the firm changed names. What did not change was driving out to the farm when something needed tending.

Five small animal clinics across northeastern Pennsylvania today. One mobile large animal team. Serving Bradford, Susquehanna, Sullivan, and Tioga counties for over 107 years.
